Bauhinia racemosa, also known as the "Aapta" or "Mountain Ebony," is a flowering plant with distinctive butterfly-shaped leaves and fragrant white to pinkish flowers. Native to Southeast Asia, it holds cultural importance and is valued for its ornamental beauty. In traditional medicine, various parts of the plant are used for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
