Welcome to Shri Ganesh Shikshan Prasarak Mandal, PU. Ahilyadevi Adhyapak Mahavidyalaya Sangavi. Teak (Tectona grandis) is a large deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia. Known for its durable and water-resistant properties, teak has been utilized for centuries in various applications, particularly in shipbuilding, furniture crafting, and outdoor construction. The heartwood of teak possesses natural oils that contribute to its resistance against decay, insects, and fungi. Beyond its practical applications, teak is esteemed for its warm, golden-brown color, making it a sought-after choice for creating aesthetically pleasing and robust pieces of furniture. Due to its versatile and enduring nature, teak remains a popular and valuable timber resource worldwide.
