Wild sweetsop or Ramfal, scientifically known as Annona squamosa, is a tropical fruit-bearing tree belonging to the Annonaceae family. Also commonly referred to as sugar apple or custard apple, this small to medium-sized tree produces distinctive heart-shaped fruits with a scaly exterior. The fruit's flesh is sweet, aromatic, and creamy, containing black seeds embedded in the pulp. Wild sweetsop is cherished for its delicious taste and is often consumed fresh or used in various culinary applications. Additionally, the plant holds significance in traditional medicine, with various parts being utilized for their potential health benefits.
