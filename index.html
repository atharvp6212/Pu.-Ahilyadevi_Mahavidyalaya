College Overview

Talking with Tree College is dedicated to fostering an understanding and appreciation for the diverse plant life that surrounds us. Our campus is a living laboratory where students can explore and learn about various plant species.

Fun Facts

Communication Network:

Some plants, like trees, communicate with each other through a network of underground fungi. This network, known as the "Wood Wide Web," allows them to share nutrients and information.

Heliotropism:

Sunflowers exhibit a behavior called heliotropism, where they follow the movement of the sun across the sky. The scientific term for this is "solar tracking."

Mimosa Pudica's Response:

The Mimosa Pudica, also known as the sensitive plant, responds to touch by folding its leaves. This adaptation is a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Corpse Flower's Odor:

The Corpse Flower, native to Sumatra, is known for its enormous size and a smell resembling that of a rotting corpse. It uses this odor to attract carrion beetles for pollination.

Carnivorous Plants:

Some plants, such as the Venus flytrap and pitcher plants, are carnivorous and capture insects for nutrients. They have adapted unique mechanisms to lure, trap, and digest their prey.
